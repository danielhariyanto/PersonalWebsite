Personal Statement #2

Describe your most meaningful experience(s) and why they matter to you.

I was a serial hackathoner. Every 2-3 weeks during COVID, I’d spend my weekend participating in virtual hackathons from all over the world. Though it was exhausting to pull the occasional all-nighter during these weekends, I’d always find myself looking forward to the next hackathon event. The truth was that hackathons inspired me. Where else could you find such creative, out-of-the-box solutions to some of the world’s biggest problems? And the fact that these hackathoners can build complete apps within 24 hours astounded me. I was so enthralled by the hustle and bustle that I competed in over a dozen hackathons during my spring semester.

My most meaningful experiences came out of these hackathons because they unfailingly show me that anything is truly possible. Any problem can be solved, any solution can be built, and it can be done within 24 hours. Free the weekend, gather an eager team, find a problem to solve; and I firmly believe a brilliant solution can be discovered.

One hackathon hosted by Johns Hopkins was particularly memorable. I was randomly grouped with two talented students, and we all happened to share a personal encounter with Alzheimer’s. Out of that 24-hr hackathon, we developed a machine learning model that can use one’s voice to predict whether they will develop Alzheimer’s in the future before symptoms arise. This idea is now a MassChallenge startup we co-founded, and we’re currently conducting our clinical validation in Sweden with the Carney Institute for Brain Science at Brown.
